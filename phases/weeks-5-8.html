Phase 3 — Layer 3 DEP Onboarding
Weeks 5–8

Execute: Own Your First Deliverables

The scaffolding is still there, but you're building now. Phase 3 is about transitioning from learning to doing — producing your first real artifacts, getting feedback on them, and understanding where you're strong and where you need to develop. By the end of week eight, you'll have a vision statement, a PRD draft, a BURP epic, and a story set. Not theoretical — actual.

The principle of Phase 3

Shipping is not success. Producing an artifact is not success. The question at the end of every Phase 3 deliverable is: does this do what it needs to do? Can engineering groom from this story set without calling you? Could a new stakeholder read this vision statement and immediately understand where the area is headed? That's the bar.

Your four Phase 3 deliverables
Deliverable 1

Vision Statement

A 2-4 sentence articulation of what your product area is for, who it serves, and why it matters. This is a forcing function — it makes you commit to a strategic direction in writing so you can test and refine it in real conversations.

The agent guides you through 5 questions, then drafts the statement from your answers. You refine it. It gets saved.

Agent task: /agent/tasks/vision-statement.md

Deliverable 2

PRD Draft

A full PRD draft for a real feature in your current roadmap — problem statement, stakeholder context, evidence, proposed solution, success metrics, dependencies, risks, and acceptance criteria. Assessed against the zone quality bar.

The agent guides you through 7 questions, generates the draft, and flags missing or weak fields explicitly.

Agent task: /agent/tasks/prd-draft.md

Deliverable 3

BURP Epic Summary

A Sprint 0-ready epic summary with problem statement, success metrics, scope definition, dependency status, and risk/mitigation plan. The document you'd bring into BURP to show up prepared rather than reactive.

The agent walks you through 7 BURP questions and generates the summary. Flags pre-BURP readiness gaps.

Agent task: /agent/tasks/burp-epic.md

Deliverable 4

Story Set

3-5 user stories for a feature in your current or upcoming sprint — in sprint-ready condition. As a / I want / So that, testable AC in Given/When/Then format, and a definition of done that's specific to the story, not boilerplate.

The agent walks you through context questions, helps write each story, and runs an engineering clarity check at the end.

Agent task: /agent/tasks/story-set.md

The quality bar — what these artifacts need to achieve

Zone standards for Manager-level artifacts

Vision Statement Quality Bar
  • Names a specific stakeholder and their workflow (not generic categories)
  • Describes a real problem, not a feature set
  • Is directional without being vague
  • Grounded in a strategic rationale specific to DEP
  • Can be said in a conversation without reading from a card
PRD Quality Bar
  • Problem statement names stakeholder, workflow, and pain point with evidence
  • Success metrics specific, measurable, defined pre-launch
  • Dependencies named with owners and statuses confirmed
  • AC in Given/When/Then format, including error states
  • Explicit out-of-scope section
  • Engineering can groom from this without PM present
Story Quality Bar
  • "So that" describes a business outcome, not a feature
  • AC testable in Given/When/Then format
  • Error states and edge cases have explicit AC
  • Definition of Done is story-specific, not boilerplate
  • Engineering lead can groom without PM walking through every card
  • No story scoped beyond a single sprint
What your manager is watching for — by level

Manager watch-fors in Phase 3

Associate
  • Is the PM producing artifacts that improve with each iteration, or repeating the same gaps?
  • Are they seeking feedback proactively, or waiting to be reviewed?
  • Are they distinguishing between what a stakeholder asks for and what the problem actually is?
Sr. Associate
  • Are their stories arriving at grooming in sprint-ready condition?
  • Are their AC specific enough for engineering to execute without follow-up?
  • Are they contributing to ceremonies, not just attending them?
Manager
  • Is the artifact quality meeting the zone bar? Where specifically is it falling short?
  • Is the PM being chased by PrOps for Jira hygiene, or maintaining it proactively?
  • Does the vision statement show real strategic thinking, or is it generic?
Sr. Manager
  • Is the Sr. Manager setting a quality bar for their own work that they'd hold others to?
  • Are they using Phase 3 to build their own strategic narrative, not just execute tasks?
  • Are they identifying process gaps and suggesting improvements?
Director / ED
  • Is the Director using Phase 3 to establish their zone-level vision, not just produce individual artifacts?
  • Are they already engaging at the right stakeholder level for their role?
  • Are they setting expectations for quality across the zone, not just modeling it themselves?
Phase 3 deliverables and close

What should be true by the end of week 8

  • Vision statement drafted, refined, and shared with your manager and at least one key partner
  • PRD draft completed and assessed against the zone quality bar — gaps identified and addressed or flagged
  • BURP epic summary ready for your next Sprint 0 session — all seven sections complete
  • Story set written and reviewed with engineering lead — engineering clarity check passed
  • Roadmap data and GA readiness fields contributed before a PrOps deadline (without being chased)
  • Phase 3 Learning Summary generated in the Claude Code agent, ready for the 60-day manager checkpoint
