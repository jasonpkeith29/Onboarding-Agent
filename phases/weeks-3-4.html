Phase 2 — Layer 3 DEP Onboarding
Weeks 3–4

Learn: The Process in Practice

Phase 1 was orientation. Phase 2 is about testing whether it landed. You've been told how the operating model works — now you'll observe it, assess what's happening in your area, and demonstrate that you can think through a hard situation. By the end of week four, process fluency should feel real, not theoretical.

What you're doing in weeks 3–4

Five focus areas

Zone Review

Attend and debrief

Attend your first Zone Review. What's on the agenda? What data is leadership looking at? What questions do they ask the team? What would you talk about for your area if you were presenting?

BURP / Sprint 0

Understand release planning

Attend a BURP session or Sprint 0. Learn what the PM's specific job is — what you bring in and what you take away. Understand how BURP epics connect to the Boulder and Feature hierarchy from Phase 1.

PRD Quality

Self-assess the quality bar

Review an existing PRD from your area. Rate it 1-5 against the zone quality bar. Identify what's strong and what's weak. Think about what you'd do differently in your first PRD.

Stakeholder Context

Know who you're building for

Map your primary stakeholders. For each: who are they, what workflow are they in, what pain point does your product area address for them? Get direct stakeholder exposure — sales demo, user research, or direct conversation.

HPCI Awareness

Learn the escalation path

Learn the HPCI (High Priority Customer Impact) escalation process and how it applies to your product area. Understand: what triggers it, who owns it, and what your role is when one surfaces.

Scenario Challenge

Test your judgment

In the Claude Code agent, you'll work through a real judgment scenario: an HPCI issue surfaces two weeks before a planned GA, with a commercial commitment at stake. What do you do, in what order?

The DEP operating rhythm — ceremonies to know by week 4

The ceremonies and what PM owns in each

Zone Review — Biweekly

Leadership review of metrics and roadmap. PM presents data for their area: feature progress, key metrics, upcoming milestones. Understanding what leadership is looking at is essential for Phase 3 work.

PM owns: data preparation and presentation for their product area

BURP / Sprint 0 — Per Release

Release planning session. PM brings BURP epic summaries — problem statements, metrics, dependencies, risks. Engineering sizes and sequences. Your prep determines how productive this session is.

PM owns: BURP epic summaries prepared before the session

Grooming / Refinement — Weekly

Sprint-level story elaboration. PM brings stories in sprint-ready condition — clear As a / I want / So that, testable AC, definition of done. Engineering reviews and asks clarifying questions.

PM owns: story quality before grooming. Engineering owns: technical approach and estimation.

Sprint Review — Every 2 Weeks

The team demonstrates completed work. PM validates that stories meet AC and are ready to close. This is the PM's quality gate, not engineering's.

PM owns: AC validation and story acceptance

Retrospective — Every 2 Weeks

Team reflection on process. PM participates as a full team member. The learnings from retros should change how work is done in the next sprint — not just generate a list of observations.

PM owns: showing up, contributing honestly, and acting on outcomes

PrOps Sync — Weekly or Biweekly

Operating rhythm check-in with your PrOps partner. Covers roadmap hygiene, Jira field completion, release coordination. This meeting should not be where you discover you're behind on something.

PM owns: coming to this meeting current, not catching up in it

What your manager is watching for — by level

Manager watch-fors in Phase 2

Associate
  • Can they describe what they saw in Zone Review and BURP in their own words?
  • Are they identifying the gap between what they observed and what they expected?
  • Are they asking good follow-up questions after each observation?
Sr. Associate
  • Are they connecting process observations to their own near-term execution responsibilities?
  • Can they explain what makes a PRD good or weak at a basic level?
  • Are they building a point of view on the current sprint priorities?
Manager
  • Is the PM developing process fluency — not just observing but understanding the mechanics well enough to operate independently?
  • Do they have a clear picture of their stakeholders and the workflows their product affects?
  • How did they handle the HPCI scenario — what does their reasoning reveal?
Sr. Manager
  • Are they assessing process quality, not just process presence — noting what's working and what's not?
  • Are they building relationships with zone-level stakeholders, not just team-level?
  • Are they starting to form views on what should change in the roadmap?
Director / ED
  • Is the Director already identifying structural or strategic issues in the zone?
  • Are they communicating a perspective on what needs to change, not just learning what exists?
  • Are they building ELT-level relationships?
Phase 2 deliverables

What you should be able to do by the end of week 4

  • Debrief your first Zone Review: what leadership is tracking, what the team presented, what you would present for your area
  • Explain the PM's job in BURP and what a well-prepared BURP epic summary looks like
  • Rate a real PRD against the zone quality bar with specific reasoning (not just "it was good" or "it was weak")
  • Describe your primary stakeholders, their workflows, and the pain points your product area addresses for them
  • Know the HPCI escalation path and who to call if one surfaces in your area
  • Complete the HPCI scenario challenge in the Claude Code agent and generate your Phase 2 Learning Summary
