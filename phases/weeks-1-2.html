Phase 1 — Layer 3 DEP Onboarding
Weeks 1–2

Orient: Context, Relationships, and the Operating Model

Before anything strategic — build the foundation. Access, relationships, core reading, and a working mental model of how work moves through DEP. By the end of week two, you should know who your key partners are, have logged into every critical tool, and be able to explain the Boulder → Feature → Epic → Story hierarchy without looking anything up.

What you're doing in weeks 1–2

Your four focus areas

Access & Setup

Get into the tools

  • Jira — find your team's project and backlog
  • LaunchDarkly — get provisioned, understand how feature flags work
  • Confluence — find your team's space and start navigating
  • Roadmap dashboards — locate the current roadmap for your area
  • Master R&D Calendar — lock in the key dates for your sprint cycle
Core Reading

Read before you do

  • Product Division Best Practices
  • Zone Review Agenda / Format
  • Product Roadmap Guidance
  • Alpha/Beta Testing Guidance
  • Any area-specific documents your manager flags

For each: note what stood out, what surprised you, and what questions it raised.

Relationships

Meet your key partners

  • Engineering Lead — How do they like to work? What do they need from PM?
  • QA Lead — How does QA work in your sprint cycle?
  • PrOps Partner — They know where everything is. Use this relationship.
  • Product Buddy — Peer PM. The informal channel for "how does this actually work?"
  • Adjacent PMs — Know who else is building in the zone
Operating Model

Understand how work moves

  • Learn the Boulder → Feature → Epic → Story hierarchy
  • Shadow a sprint review, grooming session, retrospective, and PrOps sync
  • Get a walkthrough of the product vision for your area from your manager
  • Understand which ceremonies PM owns vs. which engineering owns
How work is organized in DEP — the planning hierarchy

Boulder → Feature → Epic → Story

Boulder

The highest-level strategic initiative. Connected to zone OKRs. Represents a major annual area of investment. A Boulder contains multiple Features.

Who creates: Zone Lead / Senior PMs

Feature

A scoped, deliverable unit within a Boulder. The level at which release scope is discussed in Zone Reviews. A Feature contains multiple Epics.

Who creates: PM

Epic

The planning unit used in BURP and Sprint 0. Sized to fit within a release or a few sprints. This is where PM-written problem statements and AC live. A good Epic means engineering can size without clarifying questions.

Who creates: PM

Story

The sprint-level execution unit. What developers pick up, what QA tests against, what gets to Done. Written in As a / I want / So that format with explicit AC and a definition of done.

Who creates: PM + Engineering collaboratively

What your manager is watching for — by level

Manager watch-fors in Phase 1

Associate
  • Is the new hire asking questions, or waiting to be told what to do?
  • Are they building relationships proactively or waiting for introductions to be scheduled for them?
  • Can they describe the operating model at a basic level after two weeks?
Sr. Associate
  • Are they connecting day-to-day activity to the broader operating model?
  • Are they asking why things work the way they do, not just how?
  • Are they investing in relationships beyond the immediate team?
Manager
  • Is the PM building the right relationships proactively, or relying on the manager to facilitate?
  • Do they have a working mental model of the operating model after two weeks?
  • Have they identified what they don't know yet — and is it the right list?
Sr. Manager
  • Are they moving quickly from orientation to contribution — not spending too long in passive learning mode?
  • Are they forming views, not just observations?
  • Are they identifying the strategic gaps in the current roadmap before being asked?
Director / ED
  • Is the Director assessing the strategic health of the zone from day one?
  • Are they building zone-level relationships, not just team-level?
  • Are they signaling their own point of view early?
Phase 1 deliverables

What you should be able to do by the end of week 2

  • Name all key partners (engineering lead, QA lead, PrOps partner, product buddy) and describe what you learned from each conversation
  • Log into Jira, LaunchDarkly, Confluence, roadmap dashboards, and the Master R&D Calendar
  • Read and discuss the core documents with your manager or product buddy
  • Explain the Boulder → Feature → Epic → Story hierarchy without looking it up — including where your current sprint work fits in the hierarchy
  • Shadow a sprint review, grooming session, retrospective, or PrOps sync
  • Complete your Phase 1 session in the Claude Code onboarding agent and generate your Phase 1 Learning Summary
